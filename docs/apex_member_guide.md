### A Note to Our Community

You don't have time to waste on a wellness routine that doesn't work. Between demanding careers, family commitments, and the general pace of life in the DC metro area, finding a consistent practice is genuinely hard.

This guide shares what we see working for our most consistent members — the ones who show up three or four times a week, year after year, and tell us it changed their lives. None of it is complicated. All of it works.

---

### The 3 Biggest Barriers We Hear

**1. "I can never find the time."**
The members who stick don't find time — they protect it. They schedule classes like meetings and treat cancellations the same way they'd treat missing an important call. The class is on the calendar before the week starts, not added if space appears.

**2. "I start strong and then fall off."**
This is almost always a consistency problem, not a motivation problem. Starting with five classes a week is how most people burn out by week three. Starting with two, building to three, and eventually reaching four is how people build a practice that lasts years.

**3. "I'm not sure which class is right for me."**
Most people overthink this. The best class is the one you'll actually attend. Start with what sounds interesting, not what sounds most challenging. You can push yourself once you've built the habit.

### Our Class Guide by Goal

**If you want stress relief and mental clarity:**
- Yin Yoga (slow, restorative, held poses)
- Meditation and Breathwork
- Restorative Flow

**If you want to build strength without high impact:**
- Pilates Fundamentals
- Barre
- Yoga Sculpt (bodyweight strength)

**If you want flexibility and mobility:**
- Vinyasa Flow
- Power Yoga
- Stretch and Recovery

**If you want a challenge:**
- Hot Power Yoga
- Advanced Pilates
- HIIT Barre

**If you're brand new and nervous:**
- Foundations of Yoga (beginner-specific, always welcoming)
- Intro to Pilates
- Any class labeled "All Levels"

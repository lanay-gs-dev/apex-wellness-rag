## Expansion Feasibility Analysis

### Should Apex Open a 6th Location?

**Current readiness score: 4/10 — Not recommended yet**

Criteria for expansion readiness:
| Criterion | Benchmark | Current Status | Ready? |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|--------|
| Portfolio churn below 5% | <5% | 6.4% | No |
| No locations in crisis | 0 | 1 (Alexandria) | No |
| Owner time available | 10+ hrs/week | ~2 hrs/week | No |
| Operating cash reserve | 6 months expenses | ~3 months | No |
| Centralized systems in place | Yes | No | No |
| Profitable at all locations | Yes | No (Alexandria marginal) | No |

**Recommendation:** Stabilize the existing portfolio before expanding. The operational infrastructure required to manage 5 locations well is not yet in place. Adding a 6th location before fixing Alexandria and building centralized systems would amplify current problems.

**Revisit expansion decision:** Q4 2026, after intervention results are measured and systems are operational.

## Scenario: What If Bethesda Hits Capacity?

Bethesda is currently at 88% class utilization. At current growth rate, it reaches effective capacity (90%+ utilization) within 4–6 months.

**Options when capacity is reached:**

1. **Waitlist management** — build a waitlist for popular classes, creates urgency and social proof. Low cost, immediate.

2. **Add class slots** — extend studio hours or add early morning/late evening slots. Requires instructor availability assessment.

3. **Premium pricing** — introduce demand-based pricing for peak slots ($15 premium for 6pm classes). Tests price elasticity.

4. **Satellite location** — open a smaller, lower-overhead second Bethesda location within 2 miles to absorb overflow demand.

**Recommended immediate action:** Launch waitlist for Bethesda peak-hour classes now. This generates urgency, captures demand data, and buys time before harder decisions are required.

# Apex Wellness Studios: Procurement & Supply Chain Strategy
## Internal Operations Document — 2026

---

## Current State Assessment

Apex Wellness Studios currently manages supply procurement entirely through the owner and one operations coordinator. There is no centralized system, no minimum stock policy, and no visibility across locations. Each studio operates as an independent procurement unit, leading to:

- Frequent stockouts of high-turnover items (mats, straps, blocks)
- Emergency orders at premium pricing (estimated $500–$800/month in avoidable costs)
- Duplicate purchasing across studios for items that could be consolidated
- No vendor contracts or negotiated pricing
- Owner spending 8–12 hours per month on procurement that could be delegated

## Vendor Consolidation Recommendation

### Current: 6+ vendors, no contracts, no leverage
### Target: 3 preferred vendors, annual agreements, volume pricing

**Recommended Vendor Structure:**

**Tier 1 — Equipment & Props**  
Recommended: YogaDirect or Manduka B2B  
Rationale: Volume discount available at $8,000+/year spend. Consolidated mat and prop purchasing across all 5 studios would reach this threshold. Estimated savings: 11% annually.

**Tier 2 — Cleaning & Consumables**  
Recommended: Costco Business Center (bulk account)  
Rationale: Already using for some studios. Formalizing as the single supplier with bulk delivery consolidates orders and eliminates per-studio purchasing trips.

**Tier 3 — Retail Apparel**  
Recommended: Negotiate directly with current wholesale supplier  
Rationale: Retail performance varies significantly by location. Alexandria slow movement suggests either wrong product mix or pricing mismatch. Bethesda retail sells through quickly. Consider location-specific buying rather than uniform purchasing.

## Proposed Procurement Process

### Current Process (Problem State)
1. Studio notices item is low or out of stock
2. Staff texts or emails owner
3. Owner places order when time permits
4. Items arrive 3–7 days later
5. Studio operates without item during gap

### Target Process (Solution State)
1. Studio manager performs weekly inventory check using shared tracker
2. Items at or below reorder point are flagged automatically (color coding)
3. Studio manager submits consolidated order request via Google Form
4. Operations coordinator reviews and places consolidated order
5. Order tracked in shared system with expected delivery date
6. Owner reviews monthly summary only — removed from day-to-day ordering

**Estimated owner time saved: 8–10 hours/month**  
**Estimated cost reduction: $400–$600/month in emergency orders avoided**
